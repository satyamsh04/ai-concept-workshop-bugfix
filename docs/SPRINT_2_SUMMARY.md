# Sprint 2 Summary  
**Project:** AI Agent Concept Workshop & Academic Automation PoC  
**Team:** Sent from my AI  
**Sprint Duration:** 13 April - 30 April  

---

## 1. Overview

Sprint 2 focused on developing the **Minimum Viable Product (PoC v1)** alongside the first draft of the workshop. The main goal was to get the **core system working**, including email processing, initial AI integration, and a basic workflow that could be demonstrated to the client.

This sprint also aimed to produce a **Workshop Draft (Iteration 1)** to test the overall structure, clarity, and level of engagement.

---

## 2. Sprint Goals vs Outcome

| Goal | Outcome |
|------|--------|
| Develop PoC v1 | Partially achieved – core flow implemented |
| Implement classification | Not completed – moved to Sprint 3 |
| AI response generation | Achieved at a basic/intermediate level |
| Workshop draft | Completed, but needs further refinement |

---

## 3. Work Completed

During this sprint, the team achieved the following:

- Developed the **initial backend structure** and API endpoints for email processing  
- Implemented **basic AI integration**, allowing draft responses to be generated  
- Created a **working PoC prototype (v1)** demonstrating the core system flow  
- Designed and produced the **Workshop Draft (Iteration 1)**  
- Set up **testing foundations**, including initial QA processes and documentation  
- Conducted internal reviews and shared early progress with the client  

---

## 4. Work Not Completed

While good progress was made, some key components were not fully completed:

- Email classification logic (intent, urgency, category) was not fully integrated into the pipeline, meaning classification outputs were not consistently used in AI response generation  
- Limited **end-to-end testing**, with some instability still present  
- **AI response quality and accuracy** were not yet refined  
- **Comprehensive testing** (integration, edge cases, performance) was not completed  
- Workshop content lacked enough detail and structured guidance for beginner-level participants  

---

## 5. Challenges & Blockers

Several challenges came up during Sprint 2:

- **AI Integration Complexity**  
  Connecting classification with AI response generation was more complex than expected, particularly when trying to structure prompts and maintain consistent outputs  

- **Dependency Bottlenecks**  
  Classification is required before proper AI processing can occur, so delays here impacted overall progress  

- **Time Constraints**  
  Due to the project reassignment in Week 4, the team had limited time to fully complete all planned tasks  

- **System Stability Issues**  
  Early versions of the PoC had inconsistencies (e.g. delays, incomplete responses), which required additional debugging  

- **Workshop Alignment Issues**  
  The workshop was being developed alongside a changing system, making it difficult to keep content aligned with actual functionality  

- **Client Environment & Security Constraints**  
  Initially, the system was intended to integrate with the client’s school-based email environment. However, due to privacy and security concerns (including access restrictions and handling of sensitive data), this approach was not feasible within the project scope. As a result, the team adapted the system to work with a personal Outlook email setup instead, allowing development and testing to continue in a controlled and accessible environment.

---

## 6. Client Feedback & Workshop Direction Change

After demonstrating our progress, the client provided feedback on the **workshop structure and delivery**.

They emphasised the need to move away from a high-level overview and instead focus on a more **structured and practical format**, including:

- Clear **step-by-step guidance** for participants  
- Stronger focus on **real-world application**  
- More detailed **instructions and supporting materials** (both written and video form)
- Better alignment between the **PoC demonstration and workshop activities**  

Based on this, the team identified the need to **rework and expand the workshop** in Sprint 3, focusing more on guided instruction and hands-on learning.

---

## 7. Key Adjustments for Sprint 3

Based on Sprint 2 outcomes, the team has made the following adjustments:

- Carry over **critical unfinished tasks**, especially classification and full pipeline integration  
- Focus on **getting core functionality working properly before refining it**  
- Prioritise **demo readiness** over advanced optimisation  
- Improve use of the **Kanban board** to better track progress and blockers  
- Place more emphasis on **documentation and visibility of work**  
- Redesign the **workshop structure** to match client expectations  

These changes directly shaped the Sprint 3 plan, with a clear focus on completing core features first.

---

## 8. Outcome

Sprint 2 resulted in a **working foundation for the PoC system** and an initial version of the workshop, allowing us to demonstrate progress and gather useful feedback.

Although some core features were incomplete, the team now has a solid base to build on. The feedback and challenges identified during this sprint have helped guide Sprint 3 priorities, particularly around **system stability, completion of core functionality, and workshop improvement**.

Overall, Sprint 2 set up the groundwork needed to move into a more focused and structured refinement phase.
